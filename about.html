About

Cagayan is a province in the Philippines located in the Cagayan Valley region, covering the northeastern tip of Luzon. Its capital is the city of Tuguegarao.

Info

Cagayan was one of the early provinces that existed during the Spanish colonial period. Called La Provincia de Cagayan, its borders essentially covered the entire Cagayan Valley, which included the present provinces of Isabela, Quirino, Nueva Vizcaya, Batanes, and portions of Kalinga, Apayao, and Aurora. The former capital was Nueva Segovia.

Geography

Cagayan is characterized by its varied and stunning natural landscapes. The province boasts rugged mountains, fertile valleys, and a lengthy coastline along the Philippine Sea. The Cagayan River, one of the largest river systems in the Philippines, flows through the province. The Sierra Madre mountain range runs along the eastern border, while the Cordillera Central mountain range is found in the west.

Economy

The economy of Cagayan Province is primarily based on agriculture. Rice and corn are the major crops, and the province is considered one of the country's rice granaries. Fishing and aquaculture are also significant economic activities. Additionally, Cagayan is becoming a growing tourism destination, thanks to its natural attractions, including picturesque beaches and historical sites.

Tourism

It offers pristine beaches, rugged mountains, caves, and cultural experiences like the Pattaradday Festival. Travelers can explore its natural wonders and immerse themselves in its vibrant culture. Accessible via Tuguegarao Airport and the Pan-Philippine Highway, Cagayan provides a diverse range of accommodations, making it an ideal destination for all types of travelers.

Culture

Cagayan is home to a diverse cultural heritage influenced by the native Ibanag people, as well as the Tagalog and Ilocano cultures. Festivals like the "Pattaradday Festival" celebrate the province's culture and traditions.
